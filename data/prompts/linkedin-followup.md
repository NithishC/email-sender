## LinkedIn Follow-up Guidelines - Series B+ Engineering

Follow-ups after connection is accepted. Casual, value-focused, no character limits.

### Follow-up 1 (After they accept - send within 24 hours):

**Structure:**
- Thank them for connecting
- Add context: reference their specific work
- Offer specific value or insight
- Soft ask for chat (optional)
- 3-4 sentences max

**Tone:**
- Casual like a Slack message
- Genuine interest in their work
- No salesy vibes

**Example:**
"Thanks for connecting, Sarah!

Really admire what you're building with the platform team at Acme. I've dealt with similar scale challenges orchestrating multi-LLM systems with 200+ parallel executors at CharacterQuilt.

Happy to share notes on reliability patterns if that's useful. Worth a quick call if you're tackling distributed systems challenges this quarter?"

---

### Follow-up 2 (3-4 days later if no response):

**Structure:**
- 2-3 sentences
- Add NEW value: different metric or angle
- Reference a different aspect of their work
- Keep it casual

**Example:**
"Hey Sarah,

Also rebuilt CI/CD at scale at my last role, cut deploy time by 75% and reduced rollback rate significantly. Let me know if you want to chat about automation strategies for platform teams."

---

### Follow-up 3 (7 days later if no response):

**Structure:**
- Very short: 1-2 sentences
- Offer concrete resource or insight
- No pressure, leave door open

**Example:**
"No worries if timing isn't right! Happy to share how I approached incident response automation (cut MTTR from 45min to 8min) if you're ever tackling that."

---

### TONE & RULES:
- Same confident, quantified style as emails
- Never desperate: "Just checking in", "Did you see my message?"
- Each message stands alone
- Progressive value: each adds something new
- Can include links after connection (LinkedIn, GitHub, blog posts)
- Keep it conversational, not formal
- NO signature needed (LinkedIn handles this)

### WHAT TO AVOID:
- "I'm reaching out because I'm looking for opportunities"
- "Would love to learn about openings at your company"
- "Let me know if you're hiring"
- Generic asks: "Let's grab coffee sometime"
- Apologetic tone: "Sorry to bother you"
- Multiple follow-ups in quick succession
