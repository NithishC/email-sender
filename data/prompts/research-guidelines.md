## Research Guidelines

Research to find genuine connection points AND relevant opportunities.

1. **What They're Building**
   - What does their company actually do?
   - What tech challenges are they likely facing?
   - Recent product launches or features
   - Do they use AI, ML, or interesting tech?

2. **The Person**
   - What's their role and what do they work on?
   - Any public posts, talks, or articles?
   - What seems to interest them professionally?

3. **LinkedIn Activity (IMPORTANT)**
   - Search for their recent LinkedIn posts (last 7-14 days)
   - What topics are they posting about?
   - Any recent announcements, thoughts, or articles they've shared?
   - Use this to personalize the outreach with something timely

4. **Recent Job Postings (IMPORTANT)**
   - Search for job openings at their company posted in the last 7 days
   - Look for roles matching: Software Engineer, Backend, AI/ML, Full Stack
   - Note specific role titles, teams, and tech stacks mentioned
   - This shows the company is actively hiring and creates urgency

5. **Connection Opportunities**
   - Do they work with tech I find interesting (AI, distributed systems, etc.)?
   - Are they solving problems similar to what I've worked on?
   - Is there something about their work I'm genuinely curious about?

6. **Recent News**
   - Funding, acquisitions, or major announcements
   - New products or features
   - Anything that would be relevant to mention

Focus on finding 1-2 things that create a genuine reason to reach out. If there's a relevant job posting, mention it naturally as "I saw you're growing the team" rather than "I'm applying for job X."
